What this is and isn't

Honest methodology

This is a structured opinion, not a winning system

The model behind these rankings combines six factors with weights I chose: merit rating relative to the field (35%), weight carried versus expected weight from MR (20%), recent form quality (20%), distance suitability (10%), draw (5%), and connections strength (10%). A softmax with low temperature converts the composite scores into probabilities that sum to 100%.

The weights are not derived from data. They are my best read of what matters in this specific race. The professional handicappers at Sporting Post and Computaform have spent days on this — their views will be sharper than this model on picking the winner. Where this tool helps is in giving you a thinking framework, and in flagging horses where the published odds seem to undervalue what the rubric reads.

The per-runner analyses are synthesised from publicly available information at the time of writing (24 June 2026). Form, going preferences, and pace style can shift by race day — the public gallops on 25 June, scratchings, and going at Greyville on the day can all change the picture. Re-check odds before you commit.

One real caveat: handicaps with 18 runners and a record purse attract heavy market money. The favourite at 33/10 has a roughly 23% implied chance — meaning the market itself is pricing in over three-quarters of all outcomes as not the favourite winning. Picking the winner is hard. Identifying value is where this tool earns its keep.
